Frankfort: The Heart of Kentucky

About Frankfort


Established in 1786, Frankort was build on the crossing called "Frank's Ford". Located in the inner bluegrass region known as the heart of Kentucky. Frankfort is classified as suburban. While many individuals live inside the town, most individuals live outside of the town in the near countryside.

Frankfort is known for being the Capital of Kentucky, Daniel Boone's burial site, Bourbon tours, and southern comforts.


Attractions in Frankfort

  1. The Capital of Kentucky.
  2. Frankfort Cemetary: View Daniel Boone's burial site.
  3. Kentucky Floral Clock: A clock made of flowers and machinery.
  4. Boubon tours: Visit Buffalo Trace, Woodford Reserve, and other nearby distileries.
  5. Rebecca Ruth Candy Museum: Famous bourbon balls.

Historical Events in Frankfort

Year Event Description
1792 Frankfort Becomes Capital Frankfort was selected as the capital of Kentucky upon its statehood in 1792.
1830 Construction of the Old State Capitol The Old State Capitol was completed, serving as the seat of Kentucky government until the current Capitol was built.
1872 Frankfort Historical Society Established The Frankfort Historical Society was founded to preserve and promote the history of the area.
2000 Renovation of the Kentucky State Capitol The Kentucky State Capitol underwent a major renovation to preserve its historical integrity and improve accessibility.

Facts about Frankfort

  • Known as the heart of Kentucky.
  • Frankfort Population: 28,285 people
  • Average Income: $53,905 which is lower than Kentucky state average of $60,183 and lower than the United States average of $75,149.
  • The Kentucky River flows through Frankfort.
